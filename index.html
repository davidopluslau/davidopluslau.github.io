Welcome!

My name is David Lau. I'm a UC Berkeley graduate with a knack for software development, feet that prefer bicycle pedals to pavement, and a predilection for life satisfaction.

How I Got Here

It's been an unlikely journey. At the end of high school, I resolved not to join onto any of the stereotypical Asian careers of doctor/lawyer/engineer, and especially not a computer programmer. But it turns out, you can't escape destiny.

At post-Judo practice dinner one night, another Judoka brought up a discovery of hers: a blog from a dating website. It had all sorts of insights: women think men look atrocious but message them anyway. If you want to determine your compatibility with someone, don't ask about God and sex, ask about horror films and traveling, etc etc. I figured, this was all pretty cool, maybe I'll give an introductory course a shot. That initial interest in what I later discovered to be data science turned into one class... and then two... and then twelve. Along the way, I also discovered that I enjoyed just plain ol' software engineering. Building tools was fun. I did it first for VidCaster and then Amazon. There may be a day that I don't enjoy it anymore. But until then, I'll follow my heart.

Stuff I Can Do

My native language is Python, and I can slice a string or list with the best of them. I live and breathe Python. That said, much of my professional and serious academic work was done in Java, so I'm also fully fluent in that. I've used a Windows for personal computing my whole life, used a MacBook for a summer internship, and did all my academic work and most of my professional work in a Unix environment, so I have at least basic competency in all of them. In particular, I can out-grep almost all of my peers. I used to be a Vim snob; now I'm a Vim enthusiast (my hand-crafted color scheme, six hours of work and counting) who admits that Emacs has a lot of excellent strengths but still won't touch it. I've worked in the Django and Spring MVCs. And, I have a basic understanding of various front-end technologies like Javascript, HTML, and CSS. Though I'm no specialist in them, this page didn't (completely) build itself.

I've got some other stuff I can do, but it's on my resume. My email is davidopluslau@gmail.com (o-positive is my blood type). Feel free to ask more about my other skills, especially if you're the hiring sort :)
